Role

I led the our first mobile experience from concept to release. This included talking to users and stakeholders, ideation, designing of UX/UI, coordinating engineering handoff, and some front-end development.

Project

Mobile Homebuilding Experience, Atmos

January, 2023

Background

50% of Atmos platform views are mobile devices, yet our mobile experience was a dead-end contact form.

Direction Development

How can we provide a better up-front experience for homebuyers?

User Insights

1. Sharing

Content often gets forgotten about because it is sent to be viewed on mobile.

2. Light Browsing Experience

Users reserve big, technical decisions for sitting down at a desktop with their spouse.

3. Existing Behavior

Many of our users were actively browsing homes on their phone (Zillow, Airbnb).

Stakeholder Insights

All In One Place

“Viewing homes on desktop while communicating with homebuyers via mobile is creating a cumbersome experience.” - Sales Team

Directing Traffic to Mobile

“We shy away from email and social media campaigns because there was no experience to click-through to on mobile.” - Marketing Team

Conclusions and Direction

A mobile home browse experience will allow users to progress further through the home building journey on their own.

Approachable

Home browsing on mobile will allow users to make decisions through casual engagement

Collaborative

Viewing homes on mobile reduces friction in the sharing of listings between homebuyers and their collaborators

Designing a home browsing experience

A familiar browsing experience will allow an easier entry into the traditionally overwhelming process of building a custom home.

Familiar Browsing Experiences

Mobile Browsing Flow Exploration

The desktop experience had both homes and lots, so the flow between our mobile and desktop experiences needed to be seamless.

Cross Platform Experience

The desktop experience had both homes and lots, so the flow between our mobile and desktop experiences needed to be seamless.

Browsing on Mobile, Desktop to Review

Reserving the biggest decision for Desktop would allow for more approachable home and lot browsing experiences by focusing around engaging mobile experiences.

Home Discovery on Mobiles

To capture the 50% of users visiting Atmos, the most engaging experience should be provided up front, which is the ability to browse homes and dream.

Combination Review on Mobile

By allowing homebuyers to review home-lot combinations on Mobile, the weight of the decision could be lighter because of the more casual experience.

Final Flow

The Mobile experience was limited to home-browsing only to start, which was slightly different than the desktop experience. So, the flow between our mobile and desktop experiences needed to be seamless.

Features & Functions

The first release included only the most essential features to allow homebuyers to have a valuable experience.

Catalog

A content-rich, engaging home plan browsing experience offering a space to explore casually and a path forward.

Filtering

Easily accessible filters allow for a personal browsing experience.

Saving

Persistent across linked accounts between mobile, desktop, and collaborators.

Listing Information

The right amount of information to evoke an opinion and not overwhelm.

Final Outcomes

Time-on-site increase: 40%

Homebuyers stayed on the Mobile web experience longer because there was now valuable content to engage with

Retention increase: 30%

The value of being able to explore home plans without friction was worth returning multiple times to the mobile experience.

Released before our 2023 Press Push

This released was scoped precisely to fit into a tight development window before a large increase in Mobile traffic from Press.
